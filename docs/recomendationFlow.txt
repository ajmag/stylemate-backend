How Recommendations Will Work in Your System
Think of your clothing storage system as having two parts:

Supabase Database: Stores all information about your clothes - their color, type (shirt, pants, etc.), what seasons they're for (summer, winter), and occasions (casual, formal).
ChromaDB: Stores the "visual representation" of each clothing item - basically numbers that represent what the item looks like.

The embedding_id field connects these two databases. It's like an ID card that says "this description in Supabase matches this visual representation in ChromaDB."
Simple Example of How Recommendations Work
Let's say a user wants an outfit for a summer wedding that matches their blue dress pants:
Step 1: Filter by Basic Requirements
Your system first looks in Supabase and says:

"Show me all clothing items that belong to this user"
"That are appropriate for summer"
"And suitable for formal occasions"
"But NOT pants (since we already have pants)"

This filtering uses the fields directly in your database:

user_id field (who owns the clothes)
seasons field (summer clothes)
occasions field (formal clothes)
type field (not pants)

Step 2: Find Visually Compatible Items
Now you have a smaller list of potential matches. For these items:

Get their embedding_id values from Supabase
Use these IDs to find the visual representations in ChromaDB
Compare these with the visual representation of the blue pants
Find items that visually complement the pants

Step 3: Create the Final Outfit
Take the best matching items and assemble them into a complete outfit recommendation.
Why This Works With Your Models
Your ClothingDB model inherits from ClothingBase, which means all the filtering fields (seasons, occasions, type) are available in your database, along with the embedding_id that connects to ChromaDB.
This combination lets you filter by practical criteria first (reducing the number of items to consider), then find visually compatible matches among that smaller set.
